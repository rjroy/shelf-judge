MEETING NOTES: Fitness Score Model Options Review
Shelf-Judge Project | 2026-04-04

SUMMARY

User reviewed a comprehensive brainstorm document exploring five fitness scoring approaches for the shelf-judge collection tool: Axis Scorecard, Pairwise Preference Tournament, Profile Similarity, Multi-Criteria Utility Curves, and LLM Synthesis. Each approach was evaluated against common requirements including user-defined axes, BGG data integration, redundancy handling, prediction for unowned games, and transparency. Octavia presented the tradeoffs across approaches, with particular focus on the honesty gap—where stated preferences diverge from revealed preferences in user behavior.

User proposed a hybrid combining pairwise tournaments with multiple axes for prediction enrichment. Octavia pressure-tested this proposal, identifying that running multiple pairwise tournaments would multiply comparison burden beyond feasibility. The conversation converged on a cleaner hybrid: single pairwise tournament for overall shelf fitness (where honesty matters most) combined with direct 1-10 ratings for concrete axes like art, theme, and nostalgia (where self-report is reliable). This preserves the multi-dimensional vector for similarity matching and prediction without the comparison burden multiplication.

DECISION

Adopt hybrid fitness model direction: Pairwise tournament for overall fitness ranking + direct 1-10 axis ratings for concrete attributes + combined vector for prediction and redundancy detection. Rationale: captures revealed preference on the question where honesty gap is largest (overall fitness) while maintaining efficient self-report for axes where stated preference is trustworthy.
